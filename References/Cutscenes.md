# Start Cutscene
The Museum was built to preserve what people could not bear to lose.

---

At first, it kept only treasured things:

a mother’s voice,
a lover’s final letter,
the feeling of home.

---

Over time, it began preserving what they wanted to forget:

Some were taken from grief.
Some from guilt.
Some from people who begged to forget.

---

The Museum kept them all.

---

Tonight, curator **Elian Voss** has vanished.

The doors are sealed.
The exhibits are awake.

---

And somewhere beneath the gallery,
the truth is still remembering.

---

**You are the Museum’s newest Memory Auditor.**

Find what happened to Elian.

---

But be careful.

The only witnesses are the exhibits.

And memories are rarely honest.

# Ending Cutscenes

## The Curator Remembered

The Glass Orchard opened like a wound.

---

Inside, Elian Voss was still waiting.

Not asleep.  
Not dead.  
Only scattered across a thousand borrowed memories.

---

One by one, the fragments returned to him.

His name.  
His voice.  
The reason he had tried to expose the Museum.

---

Mara did not run.

She stood beneath the falling light and watched the collection begin to empty.

---

> “You think you saved them,” she said.  
> “Perhaps you only taught them how much they had lost.”

---

By morning, Elian walked out of the archive.

The Museum remained behind him.

Silent.

Listening.

---

**The curator was remembered.**

**The Museum would not forget.**

---

## Return What Was Taken

The archive doors opened.

And the memories began to leave.

---

They returned to their owners as dreams, voices, and sudden pain.

A forgotten face at breakfast.  
A grief returning without warning.  
A love that had never truly disappeared.

---

Some people wept.

Some laughed.

Some wished the Museum had kept what it stole.

---

The galleries grew quiet.

The glass cases remained, but the warmth inside them was gone.

---

Nothing was destroyed.

Nothing was forgiven.

---

The Museum survived.

Empty enough to be harmless.

Beautiful enough to be mourned.

---

**What was taken was returned.**

**Whether it was wanted no longer mattered.**

---

## Burn the Orchard

The first flame made no sound.

---

Then every memory began speaking at once.

Promises.  
Confessions.  
Last words.  
Names no living person still remembered.

---

The Glass Orchard cracked from root to branch.

Its fruit burst into light.

---

Mara reached toward it.

Elian pulled her back.

---

Some memories escaped.

Others vanished before they could find their way home.

---

By dawn, nothing remained beneath the dome but ash and molten glass.

The Museum could never steal again.

---

Neither could it preserve what the world was about to lose.

---

**The archive was destroyed.**

**Some truths were freed.**

**Others were gone forever.**

---

## A Beautiful Lie

The accusation was accepted.

Not because it was true.

Because it was useful.

---

The Museum rewrote its labels before sunrise.

Elian Voss became a tragic accident.

Mara Vale became a grieving colleague.

The missing memories became a temporary exhibition error.

---

The guests returned the following evening.

They admired the glass.

They praised the lighting.

They never noticed the new exhibit in the archive.

---

Behind the walls, something continued to whisper.

---

The Museum had survived many truths.

It preferred this one.

---

**The case was closed.**

**The lie was beautifully preserved.**

---

## The Visitor Who Almost Remembered

The Museum listened to your accusation.

Then the lights went out.

---

When they returned, the gallery was empty.

No suspects.

No journal.

No way back to the entrance.

---

A new glass case stood beneath the chandelier.

Inside was an object you recognized.

Though you could no longer remember why.

---

A brass plaque appeared beneath it.

Your name was written there.

---

Visitors would later say the exhibit felt strangely familiar.

Some even claimed it watched them leave.

---

The Museum recorded no disappearance.

Only a new acquisition.

---

**You almost remembered the truth.**

**Now the Museum remembers you.**
