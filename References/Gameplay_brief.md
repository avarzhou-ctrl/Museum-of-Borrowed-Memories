# Museum of Borrowed Memories

## A 10–20 minute 2.5D detective game where the exhibits remember what people tried to erase.

# Core Concept

Curator **Elian Voss** disappears during a private museum preview at 11:43 PM.

The only witnesses are seven unusual exhibits, each containing a distorted memory of the night. The player takes the role of a **memory auditor** and must inspect the objects, restore corrupted memories, reconstruct the timeline, identify contradictions, and determine what happened to Elian.

The museum initially appears elegant, peaceful, and whimsical. As the investigation continues, the player discovers that the exhibits were created from memories taken from visitors.

The culprit is **Mara Vale**, the museum’s deputy curator. She used a hidden archive melody to trap Elian inside the **Glass Orchard** after he threatened to expose the museum’s secret.

Mara is not purely evil. She believes that the museum protects memories that would otherwise fade, distort, or disappear. The player must decide whether preserving those memories can justify the harm caused by stealing them.

# Tone and Atmosphere

The game should feel:

* dreamy  
* elegant  
* mysterious  
* quietly unsettling  
* emotional  
* bittersweet

The horror should come from implication rather than gore or jump scares.

The museum should feel beautiful enough that the player understands why someone might want to preserve it.

# Game Format

The game takes place primarily in one large 2.5D museum gallery.

The player walks around the room, approaches exhibits, and presses a contextual interaction key to investigate them.

Each exhibit opens a close-up interaction screen. Completing an exhibit reveals a memory fragment and adds one or more clues to the player’s journal.

The main gameplay areas are:

## Museum Gallery

The player explores the room, approaches exhibits, and chooses what to investigate.

## Exhibit Viewer

The player rotates, opens, scrubs, aligns, or otherwise manipulates an object to uncover hidden details.

## Memory Viewer

The player watches a short distorted memory and restores a missing detail.

## Case Board

The player reviews clues, places events on a timeline, and connects contradictory statements.

## Accusation Screen

The player selects the culprit, method, location, and motive.

# Core Gameplay Loop

1. Walk through the museum.  
2. Approach an exhibit.  
3. Inspect or manipulate the object.  
4. Reveal a hidden detail.  
5. Enter the object’s memory.  
6. Restore a missing memory fragment.  
7. Collect a clue.  
8. Add the clue to the timeline or contradiction board.  
9. Unlock new exhibits and suspect statements.  
10. Make a final accusation.  
11. Choose the fate of the archive.

# Investigation Phases

## Phase 1: The Pretty Museum

The player begins with access to three exhibits:

* The Raincoat That Never Dried  
* The Cracked Teacup  
* The Donor’s Silver Umbrella

The player learns that:

* Elian disappeared at 11:43 PM.  
* Mara lied about speaking with Elian.  
* evidence may have been planted  
* Celeste Wren may have been framed

The museum still appears mostly beautiful and harmless.

## Phase 2: The Museum Starts Remembering Back

The player unlocks:

* the contradiction board  
* suspect portraits  
* memory perspective switching  
* additional exhibit interactions

The player investigates:

* The Elevator Button for Floor Thirteen  
* The Music Box With One Missing Note  
* The Velvet Guestbook

The player discovers that:

* the museum contains a hidden archive  
* Mara entered the archive  
* a melody controls access  
* visitors signed consent forms they do not remember  
* the exhibits may contain stolen memories

The museum begins changing labels, hiding details, and reacting to the investigation.

## Phase 3: The Glass Orchard

After enough clues and contradictions have been found, the Glass Orchard becomes available.

The player learns that:

* Elian is alive but trapped  
* the museum stores stolen visitor memories  
* Mara trapped Elian to protect the institution  
* the museum may be semi-sentient  
* some stolen memories were painful memories their owners wanted to lose

The player then makes the final accusation and chooses what should happen to the archive.

# Exhibits

## 1\. The Raincoat That Never Dried

**Appearance:** A small yellow raincoat displayed under glass.

**Unsettling property:** It remembers every person who left the museum crying.

**Interaction:** The player wipes water droplets from the glass until a reflected face becomes visible.

**Memory:** A figure returns to the museum during the storm.

**Clue:** Someone came back after claiming to have left.

## 2\. The Cracked Teacup

**Appearance:** A porcelain teacup painted with strawberries.

**Unsettling property:** The tea ripples whenever someone lies nearby.

**Interaction:** The player rotates the cup until the cracks align into readable words.

**Memory:** Elian and Mara argue shortly before the disappearance.

**Clue:** Mara lied when she said she never spoke to Elian that night.

## 3\. The Donor’s Silver Umbrella

**Appearance:** An elegant silver umbrella decorated with moon-shaped charms.

**Unsettling property:** It remembers being carried into the wrong room.

**Interaction:** The player opens and closes the umbrella to reveal hidden initials.

**Memory:** Someone places it near Celeste’s belongings after Elian disappears.

**Clue:** Evidence was planted to make Celeste appear responsible.

## 4\. The Elevator Button for Floor Thirteen

**Appearance:** A brass elevator button displayed inside a velvet box.

**Unsettling property:** It remembers every fingerprint that has touched it.

**Interaction:** The player repeats a button sequence heard in a memory echo.

**Memory:** Mara enters the hidden elevator while Jonah watches.

**Clue:** Mara accessed the archive, and Jonah lied about seeing nothing.

## 5\. The Music Box With One Missing Note

**Appearance:** A dark music box containing a small ballerina figure.

**Unsettling property:** Its missing note is actually a locked memory.

**Interaction:** The player arranges several notes into the correct melody.

**Memory:** The melody opens the hidden archive and activates the Glass Orchard.

**Clue:** The archive melody was used to trap Elian.

## 6\. The Velvet Guestbook

**Appearance:** A pink velvet guestbook with gold-edged pages.

**Unsettling property:** It contains signatures visitors do not remember writing.

**Interaction:** The player flips through pages and compares signatures with consent records.

**Memory:** Visitors agree to donate memories without fully understanding what they are signing.

**Clue:** The museum has collected memories for years, often without meaningful consent.

## 7\. The Glass Orchard

**Appearance:** A miniature glass tree covered in glowing fruit.

**Unsettling property:** Each fruit contains a stolen memory.

**Interaction:** The player matches memory fragments to their original owners.

**Memory:** Mara uses the archive melody to imprison Elian inside the Orchard.

**Clue:** Elian is alive and trapped within the archive.

# Suspects

## Mara Vale

Deputy curator of the museum.

Mara claims she never spoke to Elian and never entered the archive. Both statements are false.

She trapped Elian after he threatened to expose the museum’s use of stolen memories.

Mara believes memories are safer in the museum than inside people, where they can fade, distort, or disappear.

She is the culprit, but her motive should feel emotionally understandable rather than cartoonishly evil.

## Jonah Reed

The museum’s night guard.

Jonah accepted a bribe and allowed Mara to use the hidden elevator.

He claims he saw nothing because he is afraid of losing his job and being blamed.

He is guilty of helping conceal the truth, but he did not trap Elian.

## Celeste Wren

A wealthy museum donor.

Celeste wanted to recover a memory of her deceased sister that the museum had taken.

Her umbrella was planted as false evidence.

She appears suspicious because she entered a restricted area, but she is ultimately another victim of the museum.

## The Museum

The museum may be partially conscious.

It changes exhibit labels, hides clues, alters memories, and attempts to guide the player toward an incomplete explanation.

It is not treated as the direct culprit, but it actively protects its collection.

# Timeline

The timeline contains seven events:

* **10:55 PM:** Guests arrive.  
* **11:10 PM:** Elian confronts Mara.  
* **11:24 PM:** Celeste searches the donor wing.  
* **11:31 PM:** Mara uses the hidden elevator.  
* **11:36 PM:** The archive melody is played.  
* **11:43 PM:** Elian is trapped inside the Glass Orchard.  
* **11:50 PM:** False evidence is planted near Celeste.

The player must correctly place at least five events to unlock the best accusation outcome.

# Contradiction Board

The player links a statement with a clue that disproves it.

At least three correct contradictions are required for the true ending.

## Contradiction 1

**Mara:** “I never spoke to Elian that night.”

**Evidence:** The Cracked Teacup remembers their argument.

## Contradiction 2

**Mara:** “I never entered the archive.”

**Evidence:** The elevator remembers her fingerprint.

## Contradiction 3

**Celeste:** “I left before the storm.”

**Evidence:** The Raincoat remembers her returning soaked.

## Contradiction 4

**Museum label:** “The umbrella was found near Celeste.”

**Evidence:** The umbrella remembers being planted by someone else.

## Contradiction 5

**Jonah:** “I saw nothing.”

**Evidence:** The elevator memory shows him watching Mara enter.

# Accusation System

The player completes a structured accusation:

On opening night, **\[who\]** used **\[how\]** to trap Elian inside **\[where\]** because **\[why\]**.

The correct accusation is:

**Mara Vale** used **the archive melody** to trap Elian inside **the Glass Orchard** because Elian was going to expose the museum’s stolen memories.

Use selectable cards or dropdowns rather than requiring typed answers.

# Win Conditions

The true ending requires the player to:

* inspect at least six of seven exhibits  
* collect at least seven clue cards  
* correctly place five timeline events  
* connect three contradictions  
* unlock the Glass Orchard  
* correctly accuse Mara  
* identify the stolen-memory motive  
* choose the fate of the archive

The game should still allow an accusation with incomplete evidence, but this should lead to a weaker ending.

# Endings

## The Curator Remembered

The player correctly solves the case and frees Elian.

The stolen memories are returned to their owners.

Mara confesses but warns that the player has destroyed something beautiful.

The museum begins to empty.

## Return What Was Taken

The player restores the stolen memories but allows the museum itself to survive.

Visitors recover grief, shame, love, and loss that had been taken from them.

The ending is painful but hopeful.

## Burn the Orchard

The player discovers all major clues and chooses to destroy the archive.

The museum can never steal or weaponize memories again.

Some memories disappear permanently, including memories that cannot be replaced.

## A Beautiful Lie

The player makes a partially correct accusation or fails to identify the motive.

The museum accepts a convenient version of the truth.

Elian’s disappearance becomes an official accident, and the museum remains open.

## The Visitor Who Almost Remembered

The player accuses the wrong person or trusts the museum completely.

The screen fades into an exhibit label bearing the player’s name.

The player has become part of the collection.

# Prototype Scope

The polished prototype should contain:

* one museum room  
* seven exhibits, with six required  
* three human suspects  
* the museum as a non-human suspect entity  
* seven to ten clue cards  
* one timeline board  
* one contradiction board  
* one accusation screen  
* at least three endings

Prioritize a complete investigation loop over additional rooms or complex cinematics.
